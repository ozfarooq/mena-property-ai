01
The Idea & Why Now
Full briefing — read this first
The Problem
Job hunting is broken for the modern professional. A serious job seeker applying to 20 roles a week spends roughly 15 hours just on tailoring CVs and writing cover letters — before a single recruiter has even opened their application. Every job demands a different emphasis, different keywords, different tone. Doing it properly takes 45 minutes per application. Doing it lazily gets you ignored by ATS systems before a human ever sees your name.

The existing solutions made this worse, not better. Tools like LazyApply and LoopCV promised to solve the problem with volume — blast 200 applications, something will stick. The result: recruiters are now actively trained to spot and filter bulk AI applications. There are LinkedIn posts from hiring managers with hundreds of thousands of views specifically calling out auto-apply spam. Candidates who used these tools applied to 500 jobs and heard nothing. The tools optimised for the wrong metric.
The Opportunity
The GCC/UAE and Pakistan job markets are underserved by every major tool in this space. LazyApply, AIApply, LoopCV, JobCopilot, Sonara — all are built for LinkedIn/Indeed/Glassdoor with a Western job seeker in mind. None of them integrate with Bayt.com, Naukri Gulf, or Rozee.pk. None of them handle Arabic language applications. None of them understand that a CV for a Dubai-based role needs to include visa status, nationality, and a photo placeholder — conventions that are standard in the GCC but absent from Western tools.

The GCC is home to over 10 million South Asian expat professionals, the majority of whom are either actively job-seeking or open to opportunities. Dubai's tech hiring community is tight-knit and word-of-mouth driven. A product that genuinely helps someone land interviews travels fast in that network. This is our primary beachhead.
What We're Building
ApplyIQ is a platform where a user signs up, builds a master profile once, sets their job preferences (role, location, salary, seniority), and the platform does the rest. It fetches matching jobs every 6–12 hours from LinkedIn, Indeed, Bayt.com, Naukri Gulf, and Rozee.pk using legitimate job APIs — no scraping, no Terms of Service risk. For every matched job, it runs three sequential Claude AI calls: first to analyse the job description, second to tailor the user's CV to that specific role, third to write a personalised cover letter.

The user then sees a clean review queue. For each job: the role, the company, their tailored CV, and the cover letter — side by side. They review, optionally edit, and click Apply. They are redirected to the job platform with the cover letter copied to their clipboard. The entire "apply" action takes 30 seconds. The hard work — the 45-minute part — was done by the AI.

We are deliberately not building auto-apply. This is not a limitation — it is our differentiation. Auto-apply is technically fragile (CAPTCHA, bot detection, form variability), legally risky (LinkedIn ToS violation), and produces low-quality outcomes that hurt users. The platforms that built it are fighting a permanent cat-and-mouse war with job boards. We compete on quality, not volume.

Why We Don't Auto-Apply
The Technical Problem
LinkedIn actively detects and bans automation. Every Easy Apply form is different — some have 3 fields, some have 30, with custom screening questions, portfolio uploads, embedded tests. Your bot breaks on every non-standard form. Maintaining this is a permanent war, not a one-time build. Residential proxies to avoid detection cost $300–500/month before you've made a dollar.
The Product Problem
Mass applying destroys candidate reputation. In the GCC, the professional community is small — Dubai's tech hiring circle knows each other. A candidate who spam-applied to the same company three times via different postings gets remembered for the wrong reason. Users who auto-blasted 300 jobs and heard nothing churn with bad reviews. The tool that causes this gets the blame, not the strategy.
"We make every application your best application — you decide when to send it."
The painful part of job hunting is not clicking Apply. It is tailoring your CV and writing a cover letter for every single job — knowing that a generic application will be filtered before a human reads it. That is what takes 45 minutes per role. We eliminate that 45 minutes entirely. The user spends 30 seconds reviewing what the AI prepared, then clicks Apply. That is the product.

Why this hybrid model?
Pakistan and Gulf users are price-sensitive. A pure subscription sees high churn. But serious job seekers will pay monthly if the value is clear. The top-up credits are not a separate tier — they are an overflow valve for Pro subscribers. This keeps revenue predictable (subscriptions) while removing the ceiling frustration (credits). Simple to build, easy to communicate.

AI Pipeline
Three sequential Claude API calls per application
1
Job Analysis
Input
Raw job description text from the job platform
Output
Structured JSON — required skills, nice-to-have skills, seniority signals, keywords, company tone
~$0.005 per call
2
CV Tailoring
Input
User's master profile JSON + Job analysis JSON from Call 1
Output
Reordered experience bullets, adjusted summary, highlighted matching skills, added relevant keywords
~$0.015 per call
3
Cover Letter Generation
Input
Job analysis + tailored CV content + user tone preferences
Output
Personalised 3-paragraph cover letter, role-specific, Arabic or English
~$0.01 per call
Total cost per application: ~$0.01–0.03 with Claude Sonnet 4. At 50 applications/month per Pro user: ~$1.50 max AI cost per user — covered many times over by the $15 subscription.

Risk Register
Known risks and their mitigations
Job API data gaps — not all listings appear in aggregators
Medium
Use 3 sources in combination: JSearch + Adzuna + SerpAPI. Direct career page crawl in Phase 3.
Claude API cost at scale with heavy usage
Medium
Cache job analysis results (Call 1) — many users will match same jobs. Batch process during off-peak hours.
Low apply-through rate — users don't finish after review
High
Make review UX completely frictionless. One screen, one click, cover letter auto-copies to clipboard.
User churn after finding a job (product does its job too well)
Medium
Reframe as career tool. Add interview prep, salary benchmarking, and LinkedIn profile optimisation to extend retention.
Stripe payment failures in Pakistan market
Low
Phase 2: Add EasyPaisa + JazzCash local payment methods for Pakistan. Stripe works fine in UAE.
Arabic RTL layout complexity across all pages
Low
next-intl handles RTL natively. Design components RTL-first in Week 5. Test on mobile (primary Arabic device).
